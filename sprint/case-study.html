Case Study

How a 50-Person Team Saved 25+ Hours/Week With a 3-Day Workflow Sprint

Industry: Technology / Venture Capital · Team size: 50 · Tools: Slack, Jira, Email

Challenge: 49 Slack channels with no structure, 7 duplicate pairs, zero automation. Team losing 30+ min/day per person to tool chaos.

Solution: 3-day AI Ops Sprint — audit, cleanup, and automation implementation.

Result: 25+ hours/week saved across the team. Manual triage eliminated. Channel noise reduced by 60%.

The Problem

A fast-growing technology company with 50 employees and 546 registered Slack users had a workspace that had grown organically over several years. No naming conventions, no channel hygiene, no automation.

The symptoms were familiar:

  • Engineers checking 8+ channels daily just to stay informed
  • Duplicate channels causing split conversations (one pair had 93% member overlap)
  • Manual ticket routing — every support request triaged by hand
  • No channel descriptions on 44 out of 49 channels
  • Onboarding new team members took days of "which channel for what?"

We've been meaning to clean this up for a year. Every quarter we'd say 'next quarter' — it never happened.

— Head of Operations

The Sprint

Day 1
Full workspace audit. Analyzed all 49 channels, 546 users, message volumes, member overlaps. Identified 7 duplicate pairs, 24 naming violations, and 5 channels ready for immediate merge.
Day 2
Cleanup plan + stakeholder review. Delivered a single prioritized CSV: 5 channels to archive/merge, 24 to rename, 18 to keep as-is. Each with a recommended action and rationale. COO reviewed and approved in 30 minutes.
Day 3
Automation implementation. Deployed AI-powered ticket classifier (92% accuracy), daily channel digest bot for leadership, and automated onboarding workflow for new hires. Full documentation and team walkthrough.
