# LinkedIn Outreach Templates — German Market

## Target Audience

**Titles:** CTO, VP Engineering, Head of IT, Head of Engineering, COO, Chief of Staff
**Company size:** 50–500 employees
**Location:** Germany, Austria, Switzerland (DACH)
**Industries:** SaaS, FinTech, E-commerce, Manufacturing (tech-forward), Logistics

## LinkedIn Search Filters

```
Title: CTO OR "VP Engineering" OR "Head of Engineering" OR "Head of IT" OR COO
Location: Germany
Company headcount: 51-200, 201-500
Industry: Computer Software, Financial Services, Internet, Information Technology
```

## Volume Guidelines

- **Connection requests:** 20–30/day (LinkedIn safe zone)
- **Expected accept rate:** 25–30%
- **Expected reply rate:** 8–15% of accepted connections
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best time to send:** 8:00–10:00 AM CET

## SEQUENCE 1: Standard Cold Outreach

### Connection Request (300 char max)

```
Hi {firstName}, I help mid-sized tech teams automate Slack/Jira workflows with AI — saw you're leading engineering at {company}. Would love to connect.
```

### DM #1 — Day 2 after accept (The Value Hook)

```
Thanks for connecting, {firstName}.

Quick question — does your team spend significant time on manual work between Slack, Jira, and email? Things like triaging tickets, routing requests, or summarizing updates.

I've been running fixed-scope "AI Ops Sprints" for companies in the 50–300 range — basically I audit workflows and implement the top 3 automations in 5 days.

One recent team went from 8 hours/week of manual triage to 45 minutes.

Happy to share more details if that's relevant to what you're seeing at {company}.
```

### DM #2 — Day 5 if no reply (The Content Offer)

```
No worries if now's not the right time, {firstName}.

I wrote up the 3 most common workflow bottlenecks I see in engineering teams (and what actually fixes them vs. what's just hype). Would you find that useful?

Not a pitch — just patterns from working with 20–200 person teams.
```

### DM #3 — Day 10, final (The Graceful Exit)

```
Last note from me, {firstName} — if workflow automation moves up the priority list later this year, I'm easy to find.

Wishing you and the team at {company} a productive quarter.
```

## PERSONALIZATION TIPS

Before sending any message, spend 30 seconds checking:

1. **Their recent posts** — reference something specific they shared
2. **Company news** — funding round, product launch, hiring spree (signals growth pain)
3. **Tech stack** — if they mention Slack/Jira/Teams anywhere, reference it
4. **Team size** — adjust language for 50-person vs 300-person teams

### Personalization Examples

```
// They posted about team scaling challenges
"Saw your post about scaling from 30 to 80 engineers — that's exactly when workflow automation pays for itself..."

// Their company just raised a round
"Congrats on the Series B at {company}. Teams at your stage usually start feeling the tool sprawl pain — curious if that's on your radar..."

// They mention Jira in their profile/posts
"Noticed you're using Jira at {company} — one pattern I keep seeing is manual ticket triage eating hours per week. Is that something your team deals with?"
```
